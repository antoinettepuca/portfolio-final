Cooking and Baking With Antoinette Puca

Introduction

Hi! My name is Antoinette Puca and I'm a student at the University of Michigan. One of my hobbies is cooking, where I like to explore new recipes to cook on my free time. I became interested in cooking through my relatives, as they've taught me how to cook different Italian foods growing up. When COVID-19 caused us all to stay at home in 2020, I started to explore more baking recipes and cuisines outside of Italian food. Going into college, I wanted to continue learning to cook new recipes. I have found recipes that take up less time during the day, but still are very tasty. To continue exploring my love for cooking, I am a part of the club Kid's Kitchen on campus where I help write recipes using simple ingredients for food we donated to families in need. I have taken a few cooking classes with my friends and family, and would like to take more in the future.

Contents of Website

This website is split into different sections: tips, cooking recipes, and baking recipes. Throughout it, you will find images and additional advice to describe the recipes. To make this website more inclusive and incorporate different ideas, I also asked other college students for recommendations on their favorite foods to make. I have been a vegetarian, pescatarian, and diary free for parts of my life, and have included recipes for others in similar situations. Additionally, I asked students with certain allergies and other diets for recipes they enjoy as well. Note: I am not a professional chef and still have much to learn, but I hope these recipes and tips help you explore the world of cooking and baking!

Remarkable Leaders That Support Diverse Cooking Recipes and Lifestyles

Chef Marcus Samuelsson

According to this source, chef Marcus Samuelsson has restuarants all over the world. He often tries to combine different cultures into his cooking. In his book "The Rise: Black Cooks and the Soul of American Food: A Cookbook" as shown in image two of the flipping image, he highlights recipes by other chefs. Additionally, his book combines traditional Ethiopian food dishes with Haitian, Swedish, and Flipino foods. To explore his diverse and exceptional recipes, please visit his website.

Chef Tony Priolo

Chef Tony Priolo is a Chicago-based chef that owns a restuarant called Piccolo Sogno. The outdoor view of the restuarant is shown in the second part of the flipping image. In his menu, he specializes handling food allergies and being able to serve Italian dishes for different diets. Examples are a peanut free and gluten-free menu. In his restuarant, servers identify all food allergies at the table, figure out where to cook the food safely to avoid cross-contact, and makes sure all employees are aware of the allergen. The reason behind his methods is to make everyone feel welcome to eat, safe, and be able to trust the restaurant. The restaurant's motto is is "There aren't any food allergies that we can't handle". For more information, please visit this website.

Deborah Alvarez-Rodriguez

According to the website, Deborah Alvarez-Rodriguez is the executive director of La Cocina. This marketplace is the first women-led food hall in the United States. This nonprofit organization strives to help underrepresented minorities more opportunities to grow their businesses. They provide kitchen space and mentorship to entreprenuers. During the pandemic, La Cocina offered additional assistance to help female-led businesses be successful. The book "We Are La Cocina", helps highlight the work they've done for other companies, as well as offering enjoyable recipes.
